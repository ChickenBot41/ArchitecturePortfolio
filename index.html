About

I run a small studio that takes on a small number of projects — usually no more than three at once — so that every drawing gets looked at twice before it's built.

Trained in Porto and Rotterdam, I spent four years working on institutional housing before starting an independent practice in 2015. The work since has ranged in scale from a tea pavilion to a maternal health clinic, but the method stays the same: understand the site's own logic — its slope, its wind, its material at hand — and let the building be the smallest possible answer to it.

"A good plan should be legible from the roof of the building next door — you shouldn't need the section to understand it."
